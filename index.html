My Experience as an Omnichannel Marketer

EquipmentShare

During my time as a marketing professional, I was fortunate enough to work every major digital sales channel. The Director I worked for asked me to step in when a channel marketers left the company to hold up a channel, or often even stand up my own channel where one didn't exist to hand off to another marketer.

Eventually I settled into the role of Analytics manager and used my experience in every channel to deliver impactful insights into channel performance. My main strength as an analytics manager was my experience in each channel, allowing me to insightfully understand changes in channel performance and communicate actionable changes in the context of each unique channel.

My experience in digital marketing focuses on revenue-driving, high-engagement campaigns. I specialize in low-funnel shopping campaigns. In Q4 of 2024, my Google Ads campaigns averaged a 9.6 ROAS, 2.5% CTR, and 1.8% CRO. That same quarter, the affiliate program I managed drove an 18 ROAS. Combined with the other channels whose marketers I managed, our paid marketing initiatives averaged a 6.2 ROAS for the entire quarter. And that was with no organic revenue included!
